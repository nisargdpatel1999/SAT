Parts of a Sentence:

These things describe the FUNCTION of certain words within a sentence in relationship to other words. For example a noun may function as the main actor of the sentence, a subject, or may function as the thing being acted on, object.

  • Subject- noun or pronoun that performs the action
    • Parts of Speech that can act as Subject: Noun or Pronoun
    • All Nouns are not subjects
    • You can find Subjects best by FIRST LOCATED THE VERB. Each verb or action of state of being must have an actor (subject)
    • A Subject may perform 2 actions as long as those actions are not in separate clauses
    • The verb must be as close to the subject as possible
    • Ask: Who or What is doing said verb?
    • Subject and Verbs Must always agree. So we conjugate Verbs for Person (Subject), Time (Tense), and Number (Plural vs. Singular)

Mary hit Raj after she found out about his treachery.
Mary hit and punched Raj

Verb: To Be

Singular Plural
1st Person(I) AM 1st Person(We)ARE
2nd Person(You) ARE 2nd Person(You) ARE
3rd Person(He, She, It, One) IS 3rd Person(They) ARE
  • Object- noun, pronoun, or prepositional phrase that acts as an object or receives the action.

Mary hit and punched Raj

  • Words- The single smallest unit that expresses meaning and grammatical function
  • Phrases- Multiple words come together to make a phrase. COme together to act as a single part of speech (ex. Prepositional phrases) and do not have a subject or verb
  • Clauses- A set of phrases that come together and have a subject or verb. There can be multiple clauses with a sentence.
    • Independent Clauses- Contain subject, verb, express a complete idea, and can stand alone
    • Dependent Clauses (Subordinate)- Contain subject, verb, express a complete idea, and cannot stand alone.
      • Begin with connecting words: who, which
      • Help modify other portions of the sentence. You have dependent clauses act as adverbs, adjectives, nouns
      • NO MAIN VERB OR MAIN SUBJECT what the sentence is really about
      • Dependent clauses can be marked off by commas but does not always have to be, they can be surrounded by portions of the independent clause
